# Graduation Best Practices Pre-write (Electrical Engineering)

---

## 1) What are your audience assumptions?

### Primary Audience (Future Husky EE Student)

- **What they know**
  - Basic electrical engineering concepts (circuits, electronics）
  - Familiar with tools such as MATLAB, LTspice, or Python
  - General understanding of internships and job applications

- **What they don’t know**
  - Which skills are most valuable in the EE job market
  - how to prepare outside of classes
  - How to prepare effectively for technical interviews

- **Terminology awareness**
  - Familiar: circuit design, embedded systems, PCB
  - Less familiar: FPGA workflows, hardware validation, system integration

---

### Secondary Audience (Advisors / Parents)

- **What they know**
  - engineering is a stable career
  - income and job security are important
  - General EE degree structure and requirements
  - Importance of GPA and internships

- **What they don’t know**
  - Specific technical preparation for EE careers
  - Industry expectations and required tools/skills
  - how students prepare for engineering careers

---

## 2) Job title / degree

- **Degree:** Electrical Engineering (EE)  
- **Target Job:** Electrical Engineer / Hardware Engineer  
- **Focus:** circuit design or embedded systems   

---

## 3) Outline

### 1. Build Strong Technical Foundation
- Focus on core EE courses (circuits, electronics, signals)
- Develop deep understanding instead of memorization

### 2. Develop Practical Project Experience
- Build hands-on projects (Arduino, PCB design, embedded systems)
- Document and present projects clearly (portfolio or GitHub)

### 3. Gain Industry Experience
- Apply for internships early and consistently
- Participate in research labs or engineering projects

### 4. Learn Relevant Tools and Technologies
- Simulation tools: LTspice, MATLAB
- Programming: Python, C/C++
- Hardware tools: KiCad, Altium

### 5. Prepare for Job Applications
- Build a clear and strong resume
- Practice technical interview problems
- Improve ability to explain technical work

### 6. Build Soft Skills
- communication
- teamwork
- Time management and consistency
